Agree to be an attorney

When acting as an attorney you’ll need to make decisions on the donor's behalf following the principles of the Mental Capacity Act 2005 and the Mental Capacity Act Code of Practice.

You must:

  • act as instructed by donor in the LPA
  • support the donor to make their own decisions wherever possible
  • act in the donor’s best interests
  • keep the donor's money and property separate from your own (unless you already share a bank account)
  • respect the donor's confidentiality
  • let the donor and the Office of the Public Guardian (OPG) know if you no longer wish to act as an attorney
  • if asked, provide OPG with information about how the LPA is being used
  • keep records of your actions

You must not:

  • use your position to benefit yourself
  • let other people use the LPA to make decisions
  • use the LPA unless it is registered

How to agree

Make sure you understand:

  • your responsibilities as an attorney
  • how to make decisions on the donor's behalf
  • when and how you should make decisions with any other attorneys
  • any restrictions and conditions

The Office of the Public Guardian (OPG) will contact you to sign the LPA separately. You can do it online or by post.

You'll need to:

  • read through the LPA
  • sign the LPA as an attorney

If your personal information is incorrect, you'll need to contact OPG.

Read and sign the LPA online

You'll need documents or information to prove your identity using GOV.UK One Login.

If you've not used GOV.UK One Login before, you'll be asked to set up an account.

You’ll also need the LPA reference number. This will be in the letter or email you received telling you about the LPA.

Start now

Read and sign the LPA by post

If you’ve received a letter from OPG about being an attorney, it should include the paper form. If not, contact OPG and ask for a paper form to be sent to you.

Even if you’ve been sent a paper form, you can sign the LPA online if you’d prefer.

If you do not want to be an attorney

If you're listed as an attorney and you do not want to be one, you can:

  • refuse the role
  • stop acting as an attorney after you've registered the LPA

You can also object to the registration of an LPA if you've any concerns.

After you've signed the LPA

You cannot act as an attorney until the LPA has been registered with OPG.
